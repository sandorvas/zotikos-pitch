# Executive Summary

## The Challenge

Banks are investing heavily in AI, but most initiatives struggle to move beyond experimentation.

The core issue is not model quality — it is the lack of:

- governance
- lifecycle control
- auditability
- safe deployment mechanisms

Without these, AI introduces operational and regulatory risk.

---

## The Insight

AI in banking must be treated as **production infrastructure**, not as isolated models.

This requires:

- controlled lifecycle management
- clear separation of responsibilities
- continuous monitoring and feedback
- traceability of every decision

---

## The Approach

Zotikos proposes a **Control-Plane Driven AI Architecture**, where:

- all actions are triggered by events  
- lifecycle is governed by a finite state machine (FSM)  
- decisions are recorded in an audit store  
- serving (inference) is separated from control (orchestration)

---

## The Value

This approach enables:

- **Governance by design**  
- **Full auditability of AI decisions and deployments**  
- **Safe and repeatable releases**  
- **Controlled rollback in case of failure**  
- **Operational transparency for risk and compliance teams**

---

## The Outcome

AI becomes:

- predictable  
- controllable  
- observable  
- compliant  

This transforms AI from a risk into a **trusted production capability**.
